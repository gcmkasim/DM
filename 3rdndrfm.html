Vision

Vision of NDRF is to emerge as the most visible and vibrant multi-disciplinary, multi-skilled, high-tech force capable to deal with all types of natural as well as manmade disasters and to mitigate the effects of disasters.

Mission

The prime task of NDRF is to provide expert and specialized response during disasters. Often it is required to plan and prepare where disasters are anticipated, such as impending cyclone or expected flood situations.

During normal situations, the Force is actively engaged in continuously upgrading its skills via training, practice and mock drills. Its takes upon itself as solemn duty to impart training and preparedness to various civil agencies such as the police department, civil defence, home guards and the youth in colleges and schools. NDRF would truly fulfil its purpose when it is able to cultivate a “prepared” society at large. Community capacity building programs and public awareness campaigns are therefore on top of its agenda.

The NDRF continuously strives to up skill and impart the same level of preparedness to regional agencies in handling chemical, ballistic and nuclear emergencies. The training and mock drills are routinely carried out to keep the force up to speed and in good nick to handle any emergency. A fit force uses its skills to in turn keep regional units updated and in good state of readiness.

For every personnel in this Force, it is a matter of great pride and privilege that the force has been able to serve our countrymen in times of disasters and also contribute a lot in helping them to mitigate and reduce the risk and damage due to disasters. No wonder, NDRF, a Humanitarian Force, raised by Government of India, has been acclaimed to be a 'friendly Force'. Since its inception its contributions have been substantial. The force-multiplier lies in several aspects, including the technological integration adopted in its functioning as well latest equipments that are being utilized. Above all, it is a Force with a large heart with concern for all, living and suffering, both people and animals, especially because as the later constitute an important source of economic livelihood and survival for the poor in the heart lane of India.

Given its skills and uniqueness of the force it has also been used several times in resolving international crisis such as the Tsunami aftermath in Japan in 2011. The variety and task of challenges that the NDRF have been called upon to address are indeed innumerable. The mission of NDRF it to develop professionalism in responding to any such calamity and also to see how to empower the public and first responders to prevent hazards from turning into disaster. The force truly achieves its purpose by increasing its reach via training and helping various state agencies repeatedly, in overcoming critical situations. It is for this reason that the force has been stationed at different parts across the length and breadth of the country.
